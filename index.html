History of the resort:

The resort was founded in 1954. Since the mid-50s, Shymbulak has been a training base for Soviet skiers, and championships of the USSR and Kazakhstan were held on its slopes. Every year in early February, skiers competed in Shymbulak, playing “Prize of Alexandra Artemenko”, 11-time champion of the USSR, master of sports, participant in the 1956 Olympic Games. Anyone who was skiing or at least watched the training of athletes is well aware of how much time and effort the athlete spent on lifting. The pioneers had to climb the mountain for 3 hours, while the descent took only 15 minutes. Equipment came to the rescue of athletes overcoming the ascent.

Priveleges:

All benefits are available on weekdays only.

Children under 5 years old, pensioners (from the age approved by the current legislation of the Republic of Kazakhstan), disabled people: 1 (one) ticket per day is provided free of charge, upon presentation of the relevant document.

The “youth” tariff can be used by persons up to 23 years old inclusive upon presentation of an identity document.

In the period from December 19, 2022 to January 8, 2023 inclusive, the resort operates as a weekend, in this regard, preferential rates - youth, pension, disabled, etc. are not valid
